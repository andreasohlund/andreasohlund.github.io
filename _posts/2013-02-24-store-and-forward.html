Store and forward is defined in the bible as:

when the sending application sends the message to the message channel, the messaging system stores the message on the sender’s computer, either in memory or on disk

This essentially means components that makes use of S & F can continue to operate as long as you have disk space left on your local server. The obvious benefit is the it helps us tackle the first fallacy of distributed computing, that the network is reliable. Ie no matter what bad things have happened with the server we're trying to communicate with we can still continue to operate. As with everything else S & F is no silver bullet and the week spot is disk space. If you run out of it you are in trouble. Given the disk cost of today this is rarely an issue but you still need to keep that in the back of your head. On the programming side of things making use of S & F means that you can make any assumptions on the state of the receiving end. While looks like a drawback at first I would argue that this pushes you to build better and more robust system that doesn't topple over when unexpected (bad)things happen in production
